Pasta Carbonara


Description

Being one of the most beloved italian pasta dishes, the Pasta Carbonara brings families together, with its rich flavour and creamy texture.
This recipe will allow you to make this classic, crowd-pleaser masterpiece, with affordable ingredients and without taking up much time of your day.
The combination of its signature creamy sauce, crisp bites of guanciale, and the must-have Pecorino Romano cheese, make this dish a family favorite, offering a rich experience in every forkful.

Ingredients (4 portions)

  • 320 g of Spaghetti
  • 150 g of Jowls
  • 6 egg yolks
  • 50 g of Pecorino Romano cheese
  • Black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Start by putting a pot of salted water on the stove to cook the pasta.
    In the meantime, remove the rind from the guanciale and cut it first into slices and then into strips about 3/8-inch thick.

  2. Place the pieces of guanciale in a non-stick pan and brown them for about 10 minutes over medium-high heat, being careful not to burn it, or it will release a strong aroma.
    Meanwhile, plunge the spaghetti into the boiling water and cook them al dente.
    In the meantime, pour the yolks into a bowl.

  3. Add the Pecorino and season with black pepper.
    Blend everything with a hand whisk until you obtain a smooth cream.

  4. Meanwhile, the guanciale will be cooked; turn off the heat and using a slotted spoon, remove it from the pan, leaving the cooking base in the pan itself.
    Transfer the guanciale to a small bowl and set it aside. Pour a ladleful of pasta water into the pan, along with the guanciale fat.

  5. Drain the pasta al dente directly into the pan with the cooking base.
    Toss it briefly to flavor it. Remove from the heat and pour the egg and Pecorino mixture into the pan.
    Mix quickly to combine.

  6. To make it creamy, if needed, you can add a little pasta cooking water.
    Add the guanciale, mix one last time, and serve the spaghetti carbonara immediately, adding more Pecorino on top and a pinch of black pepper.
