Tastasal Risotto


Description

If you were born in Veneto, Italy, you definitely know this one. The term "Tastasal" comes from the local dialect which literally means "taste the salt", so for sweet lovers, too bad.
This is a classic Verona recipe, a simple yet powerful combination of rice, salami paste and grana padano, that blesses the tongue with its salty but creamy taste.
This is a must eat for every rice lover, and it will most likely make it in your weekly meal routine.
It doesn't require much effort and time, making it also a comfort meal for when you've had a bad day.

Ingredients (4 portions)

  • 440 g of rice for risotto
  • 400 g of salami paste (tastasal)
  • 80 g of butter
  • 100 g of grated Grana Padano
  • 1 L of meat broth
  • 1 white onion
  • 1 clove garlic
  • 1 sprig of rosemary
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 1 pinch of nutmeg

Steps

  1. Peel the onion and chop finely.
    In a large pan, melt the butter over low heat, add the peeled garlic clove, the chopped onion, the sprig of rosemary and the tastasal (salami paste) broken up by hand.

  2. Cook for about 5 minutes until the meat is cooked and crumbled, season with salt and pepper and turn off the heat.
    In the saucepan, toast the rice for about 2 minutes, stirring frequently.

  3. Add the boiling broth little by little.
    3 minutes before the end of cooking the risotto, add the tastasal with all its sauces.

  4. Stir until the meat is well combined and the risotto is cooked. Season with salt and pepper if necessary.
    Turn off the heat, season with a pinch of nutmeg, and stir in the grated Grana Padano . Serve and enjoy!
